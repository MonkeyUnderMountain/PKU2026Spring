  Let $X$ be a quasi-projective variety, on which a finite group $G$ acts.
  Then $X/G$ is a variety, and the quotient map $\pi: X \to X/G$ is finite and surjective.
  Define $\pi^*: \CH_*(X/G)_\bbQ \to \CH_*(X)_\bbQ$ by 
  \[ \pi^* [V] = \sum_{W \subset \pi^{-1}(V)} \frac{|I_W|}{\deg (W/V)} [W], \]
  where $I_W = \{ g \in G : g(W) = W \}$ is the inertia group of $W$.
  Then $\pi_* \pi^* = |G| \cdot \id$, and $\pi^*: \CH_*(X/G)_\bbQ \xrightarrow{\sim} \CH_*(X)_\bbQ^G$ is an isomorphism onto the $G$-invariant part of $\CH_*(X)_\bbQ$.
